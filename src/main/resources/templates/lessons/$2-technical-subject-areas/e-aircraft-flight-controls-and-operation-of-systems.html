Secondary Flight Controls

Flaps

Hinged panels on the trailing edge of a wing that can be lowered.

Increase lift and drag at a given AOA.

Operation

can be mechanical, electrical, hydraulic

Risks
  • overspeeding
  • failure

Spoilers

Hinged panels on the trailing edge of a wing that can be raised.

Decrease lift and increase drag at a given AOA.

  • reduce lift to increase descent rate without increasing airspeed
  • transfer more weight to wheels on landing
  • can be used to control roll
Risks
  • failure

Trim

Small hinged panels on the trailing edges of control surfaces.

Minimized control input required to operate control surface.

Operation

mechanical or electrical

individual control or connected to main surface control linkage

Types of Trim Systems
  • trim tab
  • balance tab
  • servo tab
  • antiservo tab
  • ground adjustable
Risks
  • relying on trim to control the aircraft
  • elevator trim stall
  • failure

Engine Shapes (Cylinder Orientation)

Radial

  • ✅ efficient air cooling

Inline

  • ✅ small profile

Horizontally Opposed
