Objective

  • The learner will gain understand the National Airspace System and its associated requirements and publications
  • The learner will develop skills to differentiate between types of airspace and make decisions about entry and communication.
  • The learner will mitigate risk by understanding and adhering to regulations concerning the airspace.

National Airspace System

Airspace is divided by:

  • complexity or density of aircraft movements
  • nature of the operations conducted within the airspace
  • the level of safety required
  • and national and public interest

Class A Airspace

AIM 3-2-2

Above the entire US.

Used for IFR Traffic.

Where

from 18,000 msl to FL 600

Depiction

not depicted

Entry Requirements

IFR flight plan - clearance from ATC

Equipment Requirements
  • IFR equipment
  • radio
  • transponder
  • ADSB out

Class B Airspace

AIM 3-2-3

Busy major airports.

Where

typically from surface to 10,000 msl

upside down wedding cake (shelves)

Depiction

solid blue lines

Entry Requirements

private, sport, recreational cert, or student with endorsement

must hear "tail number, cleared into bravo airspace"

Equipment Requirements

Mode C Veil (30 mile radius)

  • radio
  • transponder
  • ADSB out

Class C Airspace

AIM 3-2-4

Less busy airports.

Where

typically from surface to 4,000 agl

5 nm core, 10 nm shelf starting at 1200

Depiction

solid magenta lines

Entry Requirements

hear your tail number

Equipment Requirements

above class C airspace

  • radio
  • transponder
  • ADSB out

Class D Airspace

AIM 3-2-5

Less busy airports.

May be part time.

Where

from surface to 2,500 agl

4 nm radius

Depiction

dashed blue lines

Entry Requirements

hear your tail number

Equipment Requirements
  • radio

Class E Airspace

AIM 3-2-6

Still controlled airspace, but minimal requirements

Where

generally from 1,200 agl to FL 600 (unless specified lower)

above FL 600

4 nm radius

Depiction

dashed magenta line (surface)

gradient magenta line (700 agl)

gradient blue line (1,200 agl)

Entry Requirements

none

Equipment Requirements

none below 10,000 msl

at and above 10,000 msl and 2,500 agl

  • radio
  • transponder
  • ADSB out

Uncontrolled Airspace (Class G Airspace)

AIM 3-3-1

Everywhere else. Any airspace that is not designated A, B, C, D, or E.

Where

from the surface to where any other airspace starts (usually E)

Depiction

not depicted

Entry Requirements

none

Equipment Requirements

none
