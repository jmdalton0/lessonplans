Area of Operation II.C

Objective

  • The learner will understand airport operations, including taxiing and taxi instructions, airport signs, lights and markings, and communication with ATC.
  • The learner will develop skills to safely operate at towered and non-towered airports.
  • The learner will mitigate risk by understanding runway incursions, incidents, and accidents, and taking precautions to avoid them.

References

  • Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge

Topics

Runway Incursions

Any occurrence at an aerodrome involving the incorrect presence of an aircraft, vehicle, or person on the protected area of a surface designated for the landing and takeoff of aircraft.

Common Runway Incursions

Entering Movement Area Without Clearance

Movement Area (Protected Area)
  • the part of an aerodrome that is controlled by ATC
  • all taxiways and runways
Non-Movement Area
  • the part of an aerodrome where vehicles can move freely without ATC direction.
  • parking

Crossing a Hold Short Line Without Clearance

usually crossing a runway

Using the Wrong Taxiway

taxiing on a taxiway OTHER than the route you were cleared for

Taxi Instructions and Clearances

Don't do anything until you are cleared. If you have a question, ask ATC. They are there to help.

Anatomy of a Taxi Clearance

Segment Example Meaning
Identification "Skyhawk 123BF" who the clearance is for
Destination "taxi to Runway 18"

the runway you will be departing

or the furthest part of your clearance so far
Route "via Alpha, Bravo, Charlie" the route you will follow
Constraints "hold short of Runway 9" any other instructions

Readback all Clearances

  • runway assignments
  • hold short instructions
  • your tail number
  • (any of the numbers / details)

Important Notes

  • "cleared to runway X" does NOT mean "cleared to cross runway Y"
  • always write down your clearance
  • be ready with your ipad, kneeboard, etc.
  • plan ahead before you move - trace the route you will take and make sure you understand

Progressive Taxi

If anything happens, ask for "Progressive Taxi Instructions."

This is a service that ATC provides where they give you step by step instructions.

Request if:

  • confused
  • new to the airport
  • visibility is low
  • obstructions, construction, other vehicles, etc.

Hotspots

Non-Towered Airports

ATC is not available to direct traffic.

Pilots are entirely responsible for collision avoidance.

  • Communicate your intentions on the CTAF
  • Clarify any misunderstandings
  • Be courteous and respectful, but above all SAFE and PREDICTABLE
