Effects of High Altitudes

Oxygen

  • lower atmospheric pressure

    • partial pressure of oxygen is lower
    • reverse diffusion

Hypoxia

hypoxic hypoxia - lungs cannot absorb enough oxygen

  • cyanosis
  • headache, lightheadedness, dizziness
  • impaired judgement and slow reaction time
  • euphoria
  • drowsiness
  • visual impairment
  • unconsciousness

Types of Depressurization

  • Explosive
    • less than 0.5 seconds

    • noise
    • airflow and debris
    • fog and dust
    • temperature drop
  • Rapid

    immediately dangerous, but does not damage the lungs

    may not be detected
