Objective

  • Gain knowledge and skills necessary for engine starting, inlcuding different methods of starting as well as starting under various conditions.
  • Gain knowledge and skills necessary for performing engine check procedures and monitoring engine operation during flight.
  • Gain awareness of necessary safety considerations relating to the engine and propeller when starting the engine.
  • Gain awareness of the importance of following approved checklist items.

Safety Considerations

Position of the Airplane

Consider the spinning propeller and the airflow it creates.

  • safety of nearby persons and property
  • other aircraft
  • structures
  • debris
  • wind

Communicate

"Clear prop!"

Brief passengers on emergency procedures.

Engine Operation

Verify safe and smooth operation of the engine.

Make it a habit to monitor temperatures and pressures during flight.

Know your systems.

  • don't "double tap" the engine
  • don't run the mixture too rich

Methods of Starting the Engine

Use Your Checklists!

External Power Unit (EPU)

Many aircraft have a means of starting the engine via an external power supply (not the battery in the engine bay).

Often used by maintenance to start the engine when electrical components are removed.
