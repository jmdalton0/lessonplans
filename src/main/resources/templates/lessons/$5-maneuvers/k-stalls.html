Power Off Stalls

Common Errors

  • fixating on instruments
  • using ailerons to correct heading
  • failing to apply adequate rudder
  • pitching down to aggressively
  • pitching up to aggressively

Accelerated Stalls

Purpose

To demonstrate that a stall can happen at any airspeed.

Load Factor

When a plane has added load factor, it will stall at speeds higher than the regular stall speed.

Load Factor increased by:

  • turning
  • pitching up
  • abrupt changes to flight path

Considerations

  • excessive load factor can damage the aircraft, stay below Va
  • accelerated stalls tend to happen at higher airspeeds and therefore can be more aggressive
    (inexperienced pilots might be caught off gaurd)
  • danger of spinning

Common Errors

Error Notes
airspeed too high stay below Va
bank angle too shallow aggressive bank angles are necesary to increase load factor
pitching up too little or too slowly allows airspeed to bleed off gradually
becoming uncoordinated risks a spin
using ailerons before stall recovery increasing the AoA of a stalled wing will increase the stall
break the stall, then correct attitude
fixating inside the aircraft very abrupt maneuver, visual references are crucial
