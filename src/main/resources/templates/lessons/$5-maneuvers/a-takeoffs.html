Normal Takeoff

Procedure

  • Verify "hold short" checklist complete
  • Adjust power and control rudder to roll onto runway

  • Full power - verify and vocalize:

    • RPMs
    • gauges green
    • airspeed alive
  • Control rudder to maintain centerline
  • Rotate: Vr
  • Apply crosswind corrections if neccessary
  • Stay in ground effect until chosen climb out speed (Vy or Vx)
  • At safe altitude (about 400 AGL) complete "climb" checklist
  • At cruise altitude, complete "cruise" checklist

Rejected Takeoff

Crosswind Corrections

Side Slip

  • ailerons into wind
  • align nose with centerline using rudder

Crab

  • rudder into wind
  • wings level

Other Wind Considerations

Wind Shear

Any abrupt change in wind direction or speed.

Corrections
  • delay takeoff
  • increase Vr and Vy

Tailwind on Takeoff

Increases takeoff ground roll.

Corrections
  • depart with a headwind

Soft Field Takeoff

Purpose

Runway conditions are not always hard, dry asphalt. Runways might have a surface that is dirt, sand, grass, snow, etc.

Soft Field Takeoffs Procedures:

  • transfer weight from the wheels to the wings as quickly as possible
  • reduce stress on the nose gear
  • reduce chances of getting stuck

Soft Field Takeoff

Procedure

  • Verify "hold short" checklist complete
  • Apply full back pressure to lessen weight on nose wheel
  • Adjust power and control rudder to roll onto runway with minimal braking

  • Full power - verify and vocalize:

    • RPMs
    • gauges green
    • airspeed alive
  • Control rudder to maintain centerline
  • Relieve back pressure as nose wheel lifts off
  • Apply crosswind corrections if neccessary
  • Stay in ground effect until chosen climb out speed (Vy or Vx)
  • At safe altitude (about 400 AGL) complete "climb" checklist
  • At cruise altitude, complete "cruise" checklist

Soft Field Takeoff

Common Errors

  • failing to apply full back pressure before taxiing onto runway
  • losing ground speed while taxiing onto runway
  • failing to relieve back pressure as nose wheel lifts off, causing premature takeoff

Short Field Takeoff

Purpose

  • use best angle of climb (Vx)
  • minimize ground roll on takeoff
  • clear obstacles in vicinity of airport

Vy and Vx

Vy - best rate of climb

gain the most altitude in the shortest time

Vx - best angle of climb

gain the most altitude in the shortest distance

Short Field Takeoff

Procedure

  • Verify "hold short" checklist complete
  • Adjust power and control rudder to roll onto runway utilizing full runway
  • Apply full brakes

  • Full power - verify and vocalize:

    • RPMs
    • gauges green
    • airspeed alive once rolling
  • Release brakes
  • Control rudder to maintain centerline
  • Rotate: Vr (possibly different than Vr for normal takeoff - check POH)
  • Apply crosswind corrections if neccessary
  • Pitch for Vx and maintain until clear of obstacles (50 agl)
  • Once clear, pitch for Vy
  • At safe altitude (about 400 AGL) complete "climb" checklist
  • At cruise altitude, complete "cruise" checklist

Short Field Takeoff

Common Errors

  • failing to utilize full runway
  • failing to align with centerline
  • failing to correct left turning tendencies
  • failing to maintain proper airspeed
