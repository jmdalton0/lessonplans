Airplane Airworthiness

How do you as a pilot determine that an aircraft can be flown?

Airworthiness is an assessment of the condition of an aircraft, ensuring that it is suitable to be flown.

§ 91.7

Two Main Aspects

  • Is the plane legal?
  • Is the plane safe?

ARROW Acronym

  • Airworthiness Certificate
  • Registration
  • Radio Station License (international only)
  • Operating Limitations
  • Weight and Balance

What does ARROW check?

  • Required documents with respect to the aircraft.
  • Must be physically in the aircraft while flying.
Airworthiness Certificate
  • Given when the aircraft is manufactured
  • Saying that it is legally "airworthy"
  • Never expires as long as maintenenace is performed.
  • § 21.181
Registration
  • Registers your aircraft in the U.S.
  • Valid for 7 years
Operating Limitations
  • POH / AFM
  • Includes performance charts, standard operations, emergency operations, etc.
Weight and Balance
  • measurements used for weight and balance calculations
  • Specific to 1 tailnumber

AV1ATES Acronym

  • Annual Inspection
  • VOR Inspection
  • 100 Hour Inspection
  • ADs, SAs, SB, SAIBs,
  • Transponder
  • ELT
  • Static System

What does AV1ATES check?

All the inspections that need to be done before you can fly.

Records of these inspections must be kept in the maintenance logs.

Annual Inspection

  • every 24 calendar months

100 Hour Inspection

  • every 100 engine hours
  • can substitute for an annual
