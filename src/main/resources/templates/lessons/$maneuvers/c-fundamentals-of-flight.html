Straight and Level Flight

Might sound simple, but it is fundamental to all other aspects of flying.

Understanding and mastering principles of flight when straight and level, builds a foundation for other maneuvers.

Axes of Rotation

Straight and Level Flight

How do we know we are in straight and level flight?

Level

lateral axis

control with elevator

altitude is consistent / VSI is 0

Straight

longitudinal and vertical axes

control with ailerons and rudder

heading is consistent / rate of turn is 0

Objective of Straight and Level

  • notice deviations quickly
  • constantly make small, immediate corrections
  • monitor the results

Learning to Control the Aircraft

Light Controls

two fingers

appropriate, smooth movements

Trim

Don't fly with trim, use it to relieve pressure.

Don't Chase

make small inputs, observe result, repeat

Visual References

Look Outside!

Learn to use visual references outside the plane for attitude.

  • 90% outside referencing terrain and looking for traffic
  • 10% verifying with instruments

The sound of the engine is also a great reference

Common Errors

  • Fixating on instruments / looking inside too much
  • Chasing the instruments instead of proper small corrections
  • Gripping the controls too tightly
  • Mechanically forcing the controls instead of smooth pressure inputs
  • Failing to scan for traffic, terrain, weather, etc.
