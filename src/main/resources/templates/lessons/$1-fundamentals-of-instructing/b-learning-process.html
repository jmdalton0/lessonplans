Definitions of Learning?

How would you define learning?

A change in behavior as a result of experience.

Two Theories

Behaviorism
  • focuses on overt behavior
  • learning is a response to environmental stimuli (rewards and punishments)
  • Pavlov's dog
Cognitive Theory
  • focuses on what's happening inside the mind
  • learning is a process of thinking, knowing, and perceiving.

The Laws and Domains of Learning

The 6 Laws of Learning (REEPIR)

  • Readiness: Students learn best when they are ready to learn.
  • Exercise: Things most often repeated are best remembered
  • Effect: Learning is strengthened by a positive emotional feeling
  • Primacy: What is learned first creates a strong impression (teach it right the first time!)
  • Intensity: A dramatic or exciting learning experience teaches more than a boring one
  • Recency: Things most recently learned are best remembered.

Skill Acquisition and Practice

Stages of Skill Acquisition

  • Cognitive Stage: Student memorizes steps
  • Associative Stage: Student practices and self-corrects
  • Automatic Response: Performance is smooth and requires little conscious thought

Learning Plateaus

Temporary leveling off of learning.

Learning Plateus are normal.

Mitigate by moving to a different maneuver or giving the student a break

Types of Practive

  • Deliberate: Aimed at a specific goal with feedback.
  • Blocked: Repeating the same drill over and over (leads to good short-term performance, poor long-term retention).
  • Random: Mixing up maneuvers (better for long-term retention).

Scenario-Based Training (SBT): Using highly realistic situations to teach decision-making rather than just isolated maneuvers.

Memory, Forgetting, and Retention

Memory Systems

  • Sensory Register: Receives input from senses, discards or passes it to STM in seconds.
  • Short-Term Memory: Holds information for about 30 seconds; limited capacity (approx. 7 items).
  • Long-Term Memory: Permanent storage; requires coding and significance to access.

Why We Forget

  • retrieval failure
  • interference
  • fading
  • repression

Retention of Learning

Praise, favorable attitudes, association, meaningful repetition, and multi-sensory learning help retain info.

Transfer of Learning

Positive

Learning one thing helps learn another

slow flight -> short field landings

Negative

Learning one thing hinders another

inverted video game controls -> steering during taxi with pedals

Assessment, Errors, and Workload Management

Evaluation vs. Critique: Evaluation tests the student against a standard (grading)

Critique provides immediate, useful feedback to improve

Slip: Plans to do the right thing, but does the wrong action

Mistake: Intends to do the wrong thing and succeeds (lack of knowledge)
