Aviation Instructor Responsibilities

Helping Learners

  • learning should be enjoyable, interesting, and goal-oriented
  • instructors should not try to make learning easy, rather, challenging but accessible
  • measure against standards so learners have a clear picture of their progress

Providing Adequate Instruction

  • instructors must adapt to learners' personality, thinking processes, ability, temperament
  • incorrect analysis of a student's characteristics will lead to improper instruction
  • instructors often use teaching methods that work for them, or were used for their training - consider other methods

Standards of Performance

  • learners should be held to established standards

Emphasizing the Positive

  • learners will learn better when the experience is enjoyable
  • avoid causing uneasiness in the learner by focusing on negative or dangerous aspects of aviation

Aviation Instructor Responsibilities

Minimizing Learner Frustration

  • Motivate Learners
    • people learn better when they want to rather than being forced to
    • relate smaller steps to larger objectives
  • Keep Learners Informed
    • update learners on progress
    • remind learners of assignments, exams, deadlines
  • Approach Learners as Individuals
    • generalized teaching fits no one
  • Give Credit When Due
    • recognize high performance
    • don't give too freely or it loses value
  • Criticize Constructively
    • identify errors AND provide corrections
  • Be Consistent
    • avoid confusion
    • if standards change, learner will become frustrated
  • Admit Errors
    • earn learner respect by admitting mistakes

Flight Instructor Responsibilities

Instructors shoulder an enormous responsibility to mold their students into safe, professional pilots.

Physiological Obstacles for Flight Learners

help learners understand causes of negative factors so they can mitigate them

  • fear of small aircraft
  • strange noises
  • G-forces
  • airsickness

Ensuring Learner Skill Set

  • constantly be evaluating your students against appropriate standards
  • ensure learners consistently demonstrate proficiency in all required areas before solo flight
  • foster in learners a habit of constant improvement and better understanding

Instructor Qualifications

  • stay current on latest procedures for training, certification, and safety
  • continually become more familiar with all aircraft and avionics in use
  • retain familiarity with training techniques, certification requirements, procedures, etc.

Professionalism

Sincerity

  • be straight-forward and honest
  • do not hide inadequacy

Acceptance of the Learner

  • accept faults and problems
  • support and encourage rather than ridicule and reprove

Personal Appearance and Habits

  • be neat, clean, appropriately dressed
  • have common courtesy
  • bad habits can be distracting and lose respect

Demeanor

  • maintain a calm, thoughtful, thoughtful, and disciplined demeanor
  • demonstrate genuine interest in the learner's well-being

Professional Development

Constantly be looking for ways to improve your qualifications, effectiveness, techniques, knowledge, services, etc.
